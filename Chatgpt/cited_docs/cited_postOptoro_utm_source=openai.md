Optoro, Inc.

**Optoro** is a [reverse logistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverse_logistics "Reverse logistics") [technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology "Technology") company that works with [retailers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retailers "Retailers"), [manufacturers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturers "Manufacturers"), and third-party logistics (3PL) companies to manage returned and excess [merchandise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merchandise "Merchandise").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-1) These products, which range from [consumer electronics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer_electronics "Consumer electronics") to home goods to clothing, are automatically listed on online marketplaces, including [Amazon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon.com "Amazon.com"), [eBay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EBay "EBay"), [Buy.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buy.com "Buy.com") and [Best Buy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Best_Buy "Best Buy").[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:2-2) Optoro also liquidates goods in bulk through its other proprietary website.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:4-3)

## History

Optoro was founded as eSpot Deals in 2004 by Tobin "Toby" Moore[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-4)[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-6) while he was a student at [Brown University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brown_University "Brown University").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:0-7) The business was initially run out of an attic above the garage at Moore's house before opening a 1,200-square-foot storefront in Georgetown.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-8) Moore and co-founder Adam Vitarello, now Optoro's president, opened one of the first eBay drop off stores in [Washington, DC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington,_DC "Washington, DC").[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-9) In 2008, the pair opened an office and warehouse in [Lanham, Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanham,_Maryland "Lanham, Maryland"), where they processed goods from retailers.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:0-7) In 2010, eSpot Deals pivoted away from processing returns directly and incorporated as Optoro, Inc. Tobin and Adam were joined by CTO Jessica Szmajda,[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-10) and built a new plan to deliver technology solutions to retailers to handle their returned and excess goods. In September 2013, Optoro moved its corporate [headquarters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Headquarters "Headquarters") to a 13,000-square-foot office in downtown [Chinatown, D.C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinatown_(Washington,_D.C.) "Chinatown (Washington, D.C.)").[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-11) In June 2016, Optoro moved again to an office space located in the [Metro Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Center_station "Metro Center station") neighborhood of Washington, D.C., with double the square footage, holding around 160 people.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-12) In February 2021, Optoro shut down its direct-to-consumer eCommerce website, blinq.com. In July 2025, Optoro shut down bulq.com. In August 2025, [Optoro was acquired](https://blueyonder.com/media/2025/blue-yonder-acquires-optoro) by [Blue Yonder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_Yonder "Blue Yonder"), the AI company for supply chain.

## Products and services

Optoro's main product is a [software-as-a-service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software-as-a-service "Software-as-a-service") called OptiTurn, which is used in retailers' warehouses to sort, process, and resell clients' returned and excess [inventory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inventory "Inventory").[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-13) The software tracks and dispositions inventory as it flows through a warehouse until it reaches consumers.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-14) Using OptiTurn, workers mark the conditions of returned products as new, open box, [refurbished](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remanufacturing "Remanufacturing"), or used in good condition.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-15) OptiTurn analyzes this, along with other product information, to divert items to the channel that will get retailers the most money back.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-16) Possible dispositions include selling directly to consumers, reselling to wholesalers, returning to vendors for repair, donating, or recycling.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:0-7)

OptiTurn lists products with a high resale value automatically on multiple online marketplaces under the BLINQ brand. The software will disposition other goods that will net a higher recovery when sold in bulk to be resold under the BULQ brand on [BULQ.com](https://www.bulq.com/).[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:4-3)

## Environment

In March 2015, Optoro started a dedicated sustainability team to measure the transportation and waste impacts of the returns industry and the effects that Optoro's solution has on retailers' [carbon footprints](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_footprint "Carbon footprint").[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:1-17)

## Financing

In July 2013, Optoro received $23.5 million in [Series B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Series_B "Series B") funding from three primary [investors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investors "Investors"): [Revolution LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolution_LLC "Revolution LLC"), headed by former AOL executives [Steve Case](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Case "Steve Case"), [Ted Leonsis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ted_Leonsis "Ted Leonsis"), and Donn Davis; Grotech Ventures; and SWaN & Legend Venture Partners, which was co-founded by [Fredrick D. Schaufeld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fredrick_D._Schaufeld "Fredrick D. Schaufeld").[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:2-2)[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:3-18) Optoro was Revolution Growth's fifth investment in its "speed-ups" investment fund, which was created to support the growth of newly formed companies and to widen the audience for their products.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-:3-18)

In December 2014, Optoro closed $50 million in funding in a [Series C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Series_C "Series C") round led by [Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kleiner_Perkins_Caufield_&_Byers "Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers"), a Silicon Valley [venture capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venture_capital "Venture capital") firm, as well as [Generation Investment Management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_Investment_Management "Generation Investment Management"), a VC company founded by [Al Gore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Gore "Al Gore").[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-19) The financing from KPCB came from its Green Growth Fund.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-20)

In July 2015, Optoro received $40 million in debt financing from TriplePoint Venture Growth and Square 1 Bank to support scaling its software and its consumer base.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-21)

In December 2016, Optoro raised $30 million in Series D funding from UPS, Revolution Growth, Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, Generation Investment Management, Tenfore Holdings, SWaN and Legend Venture Partners and the Maryland Venture Fund.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optoro#cite_note-22)
