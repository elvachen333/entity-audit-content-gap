When a package arrives at your mailroom, reception desk, or loading dock, TekTrack helps log it in quickly and efficiently. Staff scan the carrier label with any compatible scanner, phone, or tablet. Common Questions

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is mailroom management software?

Mailroom management software is a digital system that manages inbound packages and mail from the moment they arrive at your facility to the moment they are delivered or picked up by the recipient. It replaces paper logs, spreadsheets, and manual notification processes with an automated workflow that captures carrier data, sends recipient notifications, stores a searchable delivery record, and confirms receipt with a digital signature or photo. TekTrack is purpose-built for this workflow and serves as a complete mailroom system for any size organization.

### Does TekTrack require special hardware?

No. TekTrack works with most standard barcode scanners, as well as phones and tablets running Windows, iOS, or Android. If your organization already has compatible hardware, TekTrack can use it. If you need to source hardware, TekCore can help you identify the right equipment for your environment. Dedicated scanners, mobile devices, signature pads, and label printers are all supported.

### How does TekTrack notify recipients?

TekTrack supports email, SMS, WhatsApp, HTTP web service posts, and custom notification channels built through a plugin architecture. Every notification template is fully customizable: you control the content, the data fields that appear, when the notification sends, and who receives it. Notifications fire automatically when a package is processed, with no additional step required from your staff.

### Can I use the software in dead spots on campus?

Yes. TekTrack works with or without a network connection. Staff can scan labels, log packages, capture signatures, and take photos whether they are online or completely offline-no Wi-Fi, no cellular, no internet required. Everything recorded during an offline session syncs automatically the moment a connection is available. This makes TekTrack reliable in basements, loading docks, remote buildings, and anywhere else your campus network does not reach.

### Can TekTrack handle multiple mailrooms and campuses?

Yes. TekTrack manages packages across multiple mailrooms, buildings, campuses, and facilities from a single platform. Managers can see what is happening across the entire operation from one dashboard, not just in one location. This is particularly valuable for universities with packages arriving at multiple campus buildings, corporate campuses with several receiving points, and organizations operating across multiple sites.

### Does TekTrack integrate with our existing directory?

Yes. TekTrack integrates with Active Directory, LDAP, and other organizational directories so that recipient identification is automatic when a package label is scanned. It also integrates with ERP systems, locker systems, and property management platforms. TekTrack assists in matching the recipient from your existing directory without requiring staff to search or type anything.

### How does TekTrack help with package disputes?

Every package in TekTrack has a complete, detailed record: arrival time, carrier, tracking number, storage location, who was notified and when, delivery time, and the recipient’s digital signature and proof-of-delivery photo. When a dispute arises, you can retrieve the full package history in seconds and provide documented proof of exactly what happened at every step. No more relying on memory or hoping a paper log entry is legible.

### Is TekTrack available as cloud or on-premise?

Both. TekTrack is available as a cloud-hosted solution or as an on-premise installation on your own infrastructure. Cloud customers benefit from automatic updates, no server maintenance, and access from anywhere. On-premise customers benefit from full data control, operation within their own network, and the ability to work without internet access. Your TekCore representative can help you determine which deployment model fits your organization’s requirements.
