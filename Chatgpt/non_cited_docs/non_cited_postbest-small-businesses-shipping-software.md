## **What is shipping software?**

Shipping software is used to streamline the process of shipping goods. It can automate many tasks, such as labeling packages, calculating postage, and generating reports.

Shipping software can also help businesses keep track of their inventory and budget for shipping costs. Many companies use shipping software to improve their customer service and reduce their shipping expenses.

## **What can shipping software do for your small businesses?**

The cost of shipping software can vary depending on a few factors, such as the size of your business, the features you need, and whether you want to purchase a subscription or a one-time license.

Generally speaking, subscription-based shipping software can cost anywhere from $20 to $100 per month, while one-time licensed software can cost upwards of $500.

### **Reduced shipping rates**

If you own a small business, you know how important it is to keep your shipping costs down. But with the constantly rising shipping prices, it can be hard to keep your rates low without sacrificing quality or service.

Fortunately, there are some ways that you can reduce your shipping costs without compromising on either of those things.

One way is to use shipping software. Shipping software can help you automate the process of shipping, from calculating rates to printing labels. This can save you a lot of time and money, as well as make sure that your shipments are always accurate.

### **Easily manage inventory**

It’s easy to get overwhelmed when managing inventory for your small business. There are so many moving parts, and it can be tough to keep track of everything. But with the right shipping software in place, you can streamline the process and simplify it.

Shipping software allows you to manage inventory across all your sales channels in one place. You can see what’s in stock, what needs to be ordered, and where it’s all located. This makes it easy to keep track of your inventory and ensure that you always have the products your customers need.

### **Data consolidation**

Data consolidation is gathering all your disparate data sources into one central location. This can be a daunting task, especially if you don’t have a lot of IT resources at your disposal.

There are a few different approaches you can take when consolidating your data. One option is to use a data warehouse solution like Microsoft SQL Server. This option can be expensive, however, and may require more IT resources than you have available.

### **Real time tracking**

[Real-time tracking](https://www.detrack.com/blog/a-complete-guide-how-do-companies-track-packages/) can be extremely beneficial for several reasons.

Firstly, it allows businesses to see exactly where their shipments are at any given time, which can be invaluable when trying to keep track of inventory levels.

Secondly, it helps to ensure that shipments are delivered on time, as businesses can see exactly when they are due to arrive.

Finally, it can help to reduce the overall costs of shipping, as businesses can track their shipments and ensure that they are taking the most efficient route possible.
