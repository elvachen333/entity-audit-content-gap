## **Other Notable Shopify Shipping Apps**

### **ShipStation – For Businesses with Complex Needs**

**Quick Overview:** A feature-rich platform built for large operations with complex shipping needs and multiple warehouses.

**Useful For:** Businesses with complex shipping needs.

**Key Strength:** Offers robust automation, flexible shipping rules, and strong inventory management across multiple locations.

**Main Weakness:** May feel overwhelming or cost-prohibitive for smaller operations due to its pricing tiers and feature complexity. (The free plan allows only up to 10 labels, with paid plans starting at $9.99/month. API access is available only on higher-tier plans.)

**Shopify Integration:** Integration works well overall, though some merchants have expressed concerns about recent policy changes.

**When to Choose:** A good fit if you need advanced control over every aspect of shipping. Smaller businesses often find that Shippo provides similar functionality at a more accessible cost and easier to use.

### **Pirate Ship – Best Free Option (With Significant Limitations)**

**Quick Overview:** A completely free shipping platform that works exclusively with USPS and UPS carriers.

**Best For:** Very small businesses with straightforward needs that don’t anticipate expanding beyond domestic USPS or UPS shipping or needing integrations with other major e-commerce marketplaces/platforms, like Etsy.

**Key Strength:** No monthly fees—entirely free to use, making it appealing for those just starting out.

**Main Weakness:** Limited primarily to USPS and UPS, with fewer store integrations and scaling options compared to some competitors.

**Shopify Integration:** Offers a simple integration with Shopify, though many tasks still require manual setup or management.

**When to Choose:** A reasonable choice if keeping costs at zero is your top priority and your shipping needs are minimal. Businesses that grow or require more carrier flexibility typically find themselves outgrowing these limitations fairly quickly.

### **Veeqo – Best for Amazon-Primary Sellers**

**Quick Overview:** An Amazon-owned platform built with a strong focus on inventory management and deep Amazon integration.

**Most Compatible:** Businesses that rely mainly on Amazon Marketplace sales and use Shopify as a secondary channel.

**Key Feature:** Alignment with Amazon Seller Central and reliable multi-channel inventory management.

**Main Weakness:** Because the platform is owned by Amazon, merchant data falls under Amazon’s broader privacy and data policies, which may be a concern from some users who don’t want to potentially share customer data with Amazon.

**Shopify Integration:** Works smoothly with Shopify overall, though its design and features clearly prioritize Amazon, which can make Shopify feel less central to the experience.

**When to Choose:** A solid choice if Amazon is your primary sales channel and you’re comfortable operating within Amazon’s ecosystem. If Shopify is the core of your business, you may find other platforms better aligned with your priorities.

### **ShippingEasy – Best for Built-In Email Marketing**

**Quick Overview:** A shipping platform that combines fulfillment tools with built-in email marketing features.

**Best For:** Suitable for businesses at various stages, especially those looking to blend shipping and marketing efforts in one place.

**Key Strength:** Convenient integration of email marketing tools directly within the shipping platform, simplifying customer communication and follow-ups.

**Main Weakness:** Limited carrier support (USPS, UPS, FedEx, with no FedEx discounts) and marketing capabilities that don’t quite match specialized platforms like Klaviyo. Pricing can also increase once marketing features are added.

**Shopify Integration:** Integrates smoothly with Shopify, particularly for merchants looking to connect shipping workflows with marketing automation.

**When to Choose:** A good option if you value having email marketing and shipping in one system and don’t mind paying extra for the convenience, even if dedicated marketing tools offer deeper functionality.

### **ShipSaving – Budget Alternative for Ultra-Basic Needs**

**Quick Overview:** A relatively new platform (founded in 2019) that focuses on affordability and simplicity.

**Best For:** Startups or small businesses operating on very tight budgets with straightforward shipping needs.

**Key Strength:** Offers low per-label pricing and helpful features like QR code labels for users who don’t have printers.

**Main Weakness:** As a newer platform, it has a smaller user base and fewer advanced tools compared to more established competitors. The feature set continues to grow, but it may feel limited for those needing robust automation or analytics. Simply put: Shipsaving doesn’t have the proven scalability of other users.

**Shopify Integration:** Provides a simple connection with Shopify, though its other store integrations and capabilities are still maturing.

**When to Choose:** A practical choice if minimizing shipping costs is your top priority and you’re comfortable with a platform that’s still developing. Businesses that need more advanced features or proven scalability may prefer a more established option.
