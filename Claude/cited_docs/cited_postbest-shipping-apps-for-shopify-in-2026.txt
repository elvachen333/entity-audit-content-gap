Best Shipping Apps for Shopify in 2026

By \
\
Shippo

Shopify includes built-in shipping features, but the right shipping app can unlock better discounted rates, automations, and advanced capabilities that save you even more time and money. Whether you're processing ten orders a week or a thousand a day, these shipping apps help you scale efficiently.

Why Add a Shipping App to Your Shopify Store

Shopify's native shipping works well for basic needs, but growing businesses quickly outpace  these features. A shipping app extends your capabilities in several important ways.

You gain access to:

Discounted rates from multiple international, domestic, and regional carriers

Branded tracking pages

Seamless returns

Multi-channel order consolidation

Team shipping

Advanced automations

The combination of time-savings, cost-savings and an elevated customer experience alone justify the investment.

Top Shopify Shipping Apps

Shippo

Shippo offers straightforward shipping for Shopify merchants who want ease-of-use without sacrificing functionality. It brings orders from multiple stores into one centralized dashboard and helps automate your shipping to save time and effort. The interface is clean and easy to navigate, making it accessible even if you're new to shipping.

Key Features:

Deep discounted rates for 
40 global carriers

Multi-channel support (e.g., connect all your stores like Shopify, Etsy, Amazon, and more to Shippo)

Real-time 
shipping rates

AI-powered delivery date estimates

Branded tracking pages

Seamless returns

Simplified international shipping

Bulk label editing and printing for up to 100 orders at once

Configurable automations

Shipping insurance

Manifests

Scheduled pickups

Suitable For:
 Businesses of all sizes that want affordability without having to invest in a steep monthly commitment.

Pricing:
 
Starter plan is free up to 30 labels
. Professional plan starts at $19/month depending on monthly label volume.

ShipStation

It handles complex multi-channel operations, consolidating orders from top e-commerce marketplaces/platforms and other niche platforms into a unified dashboard.

Shipstation offers robust automations, and might be well suited for your business if you have complex shipping needs.

Key Features:

Multi-carrier support (USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, and more)

Discounted shipping rates

Batch label printing

Custom automation rules

Multi-channel order management

Branded packing slips and tracking

Real-time inventory syncing

Suitable For:
 Businesses with complex shipping needs.

Pricing:
 Plans start on their free tier for up to 10 shipments. In order to access their API features, you must be on their $29.99/month plan for up to 50 shipments.

Bottom Line:
 While powerful, their tiered pricing structure means you may have to pay a significantly higher monthly fee just to access essential features like API integration.

Easyship

Easyship specializes in global shipping, making international fulfillment accessible for stores of any size. The platform connects you with 550+ courier services worldwide and provides pre-negotiated rates that can save up to 91% off standard pricing.

The international capabilities include automatic customs documentation, real-time duty and tax calculations, and the ability to display landed costs at checkout. This transparency helps convert international customers who might otherwise abandon their carts due to unexpected fees.

Key Features:

550+ courier services globally

Automated customs documentation

Tax and duty calculations at checkout

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) and DDU options

Smart box selection

Branded tracking and returns

Shipping rules automation

Suitable For:
 Stores that ship primarily internationally.

Pricing:
 Free plan for up to 50 shipments/month. Plus plan at $29/month, Premier at $69/month, and Scale at $99/month.

Bottom Line:
 Their platform is highly specialized for international logistics, which may feel overly complex or unnecessary for merchants primarily focused on domestic growth.

AfterShip

AfterShip focuses on the post-purchase experience, providing tracking and notifications that keep customers informed throughout delivery.

You can customize tracking pages with your branding, add product recommendations, and include promotional banners. The automated notifications via email, SMS, and push notifications reduce "where is my order" inquiries.

Key Features:

Tracking for 600+ carriers worldwide

Branded tracking pages

Automated email, SMS, and push notifications

Product recommendations on tracking pages

Delivery analytics and insights

Returns management portal

Integration with customer support tools

Best For:
 Stores focused on customer experience and reducing support inquiries about order status.

Pricing:
 Paid plans start at $11/month for up to 100 shipments.

Bottom Line
: Because their focus is on post-purchase tracking and notifications, you will likely still need to find a separate solution to handle your actual label generation.

ShippingEasy

ShippingEasy combines shipping automation with marketing tools, helping you not only ship efficiently but also drive repeat purchases. The platform includes email marketing features that send automated campaigns based on customer behavior.

The inventory management features help prevent overselling across multiple channels. You can set low-stock alerts and track inventory levels in real-time, which becomes crucial as your product catalog grows.

Key Features:

Multi-carrier rate comparison

Discounted shipping rates

Automated email marketing

Customer segmentation

Inventory management

Branded packing slips

Return labels

Suitable For:
 Growing businesses that want shipping combined with marketing automation.

Pricing:
 Free plan up to 25 shipments a month. Growth plan starts at $19.99 a month for up to 200 shipments.

Bottom Line:
  By bundling shipping with email marketing and inventory tools, you may end up paying for extra features that overlap with apps you already use on Shopify.

ShipperHQ

ShipperHQ provides advanced rate calculation and checkout customization. If your products have complex shipping requirements—like dimensional weight, multi-origin shipping, or LTL freight—this app handles them elegantly.

The checkout experience rivals Amazon's, with accurate delivery date estimates, multiple shipping options clearly presented, and the ability to create custom rules based on product attributes, customer location, or order value.

Key Features:

Real-time rates from 50+ carriers

Advanced rate calculation rules

Dimensional packing

Multi-origin shipping

LTL freight support

Delivery date and time selection

In-store pickup

Address validation

Suitable For:
 Stores with complex shipping needs, oversized items, or multi-warehouse operations.

Pricing:
 Plan rates are hidden on the website but can range up to $350 dollars a month plus more for enterprise level pricing.

Bottom Line:
 Their specialized focus on complex freight and dimensional rules often comes with a much higher price point and less transparent pricing than most Shopify merchants require.

ReadyCloud

ReadyCloud provides an all-in-one solution combining CRM, shipping, and returns management. The platform turns order data into customer profiles complete with purchase history, preferences, and communication logs.

The integrated approach means less switching between tools. You can manage shipping, handle returns, send marketing campaigns, and track customer interactions from a single dashboard.

Key Features:

Multi-carrier shipping automation

Returns management

CRM with customer profiles

Email and SMS marketing

Order syncing across channels

Team collaboration tools

Action alerts and automation

Suitable For:
 Growing businesses that want unified operations across shipping, customer service, and marketing.

Pricing:
 $24/month per user. Additional fees for shipping and returns features.

Bottom Line:
 Their per-user pricing model can become unexpectedly expensive and difficult to scale as your team grows and you add more shipping and return capabilities.

Native Shopify Shipping

Shopify's built-in shipping deserves mention. For U.S.-based merchants, Shopify Shipping provides access to USPS, FedEx, UPS, and DHL rates with significant discounts (up to 88% off for certain services).

You can print labels directly from your Shopify admin without additional apps. While it lacks advanced features like automation rules or multi-channel consolidation, it works well for straightforward shipping needs.

Suitable For:
 New Shopify stores with simple shipping requirements.

Pricing:
 Included with Shopify subscription. Pay only for postage at discounted rates.

Bottom Line
: While convenient for beginners, the lack of advanced automation and multi-channel support means you will likely outgrow its basic functionality very quickly.

Choosing the Right Shipping App

Your ideal shipping app depends on several factors:

Business Size:
 Shippo makes the most sense for businesses of all sizes because of their affordable plans across all sizes and the fact that all features, like API access is available on all plans.

Shipping Destinations:
 Where you are planning to ship can help you figure out who the right partner is. You’ll want to look for a partner that is flexible enough to adapt with you.

Product Complexity:
 If your business has complex shipping needs, then you need a partner with advanced shipping capabilities like Shippo or Shipstation.

Budget:
 Factor in both subscription fees and potential savings from discounted rates. Sometimes a more expensive app saves you more time and money overall through better time-savings tools and discounted carrier rates.

Feature Requirements:
 Determine what matters most—automation, tracking, returns management, or marketing integration—then choose accordingly.
