What is Package Tracking Software?

Package tracking software connects to carrier APIs to retrieve real-time tracking data across multiple carriers simultaneously. At its most basic, it answers the question: where is this package right now? At its most capable, it uses that tracking data to drive proactive customer communication, reduce delivery exceptions through early detection, create branded tracking experiences that replace generic carrier pages, and generate carrier performance analytics for operations and finance teams.

For e-commerce brands, the practical use cases break into three layers: customer-facing (branded tracking pages, automated shipping notifications, and self-serve order lookup); internal operations (exception alerts, delivery performance dashboards, and multi-carrier shipment visibility); and commercial (carrier performance data for contract negotiations, WISMO reduction metrics, and post-purchase revenue opportunities on the tracking page).

The tools below span all three layers to varying degrees. Not every team needs all three, but understanding which layer you’re buying is essential before committing to a platform.

Key Features to Look for in Package Tracking Software

Package tracking software is packed with features designed to make logistics smoother and deliveries more reliable. Here’s a look at the core features that define a package tracking system.

Package tracking in real time

Package tracking software offers real-time package tracking. This allows you to stay on top of your shipping, helps customers stay updated on delivery ETAs, and facilitates a positive post-purchase experience. In case of delays, you can quickly contact the carrier partner to address the issue and bring the package back on track.

Proactive and Regular Shipping Updates

80% of customers expect regular shipping updates
 or they will switch brands. That’s a real threat. Fortunately, you have package tracking software to manage this. These solutions share regular notifications when things are smooth and proactive alerts when things go wrong with you and your customers to keep all stakeholders updated on package statuses at all times.

Multi-carrier support

Package tracking software offers multiple carrier and communication support so that you have the flexibility to select the carrier partner that gives you the best terms. This feature also makes switching carriers easy, should you need to.

Analytics and reports

Package tracking software offers robust reporting and analytics that help you monitor your on-time delivery performance and that of your carrier partners. This insight helps make informed decisions regarding the renewal of carrier contracts, budgets, etc.

Seamless integration with marketing automation tools

Lastly, package tracking tools seamlessly integrate with marketing automation tools such as Klaviyo and Attentive to facilitate easy order status communication with customers across all stages of the customer journey, particularly in the most opaque post-purchase phase.

How Do You Choose the Right Package Tracking Software?

The right package tracking software is the one that solves the specific problem you’re trying to fix, not the one with the most features. Here is how to narrow down:

What is your primary pain point: WISMO volume, exception handling, carrier visibility, or brand experience?

If WISMO volume is the problem, the most impactful features are self-serve order lookup and proactive exception notifications. Both directly remove the reason customers contact support. If carrier visibility is the issue (you can’t see shipments across your full carrier mix in one place), carrier coverage and multi-carrier dashboard are the features to evaluate first. If brand experience is the driver, branded tracking page depth and notification customization are the primary criteria.

How does the platform's pricing structure fit your shipment volume?

Per-shipment pricing scales linearly with your volume. Fixed monthly pricing has a ceiling where it becomes cheaper, and a floor where per-shipment pricing wins. For brands shipping fewer than 500 shipments per month, fixed plans are often cheaper. Above 2,000-3,000 shipments per month, per-shipment models like LateShipment.com’s typically cost less. Run the math on your actual monthly shipment volume before comparing headline prices.

Does the platform connect to your existing stack?

The integration that matters most is your e-commerce platform (Shopify, Magento, WooCommerce, BigCommerce) and your marketing automation tool (Klaviyo, Attentive). A tracking platform that doesn’t connect natively to both means your notifications go out from a generic sending domain and your tracking data doesn’t sync to your customer records. Ask every vendor to demo the specific integration with your platform before shortlisting.

What do you need beyond tracking?

If your team currently manages tracking, returns, shipping refunds, and shipping insurance across four separate tools, consolidation onto a single platform reduces integration overhead significantly. LateShipment.com is the only platform on this list that covers all four in a single suite. If you only need tracking and notifications, many of the other platforms on this list handle that more economically.

How scalable is the platform as your order volume grows?

A platform that works well at 500 shipments per month may not perform at 5,000. Ask vendors specifically about performance at your target growth volume: does the dashboard load in real time at scale, does the API rate-limit on high-volume requests, and are there overage charges that significantly change the economics at higher volume?

Package tracking software automates your package tracking, allowing e-commerce businesses and customers to monitor and track the status of their packages from dispatch to delivery. It offers features such as real-time package tracking, package status updates, multiple carrier support, a user-friendly interface, seamless integration with business systems, and insightful data and reports.

While the ‘best’ package tracking software will depend on your unique needs, LateShipment.com’s software is definitely one to explore. What are the benefits of package tracking software?

The benefits of package tracking software include cost reduction, improved customer experience, package route analysis, reduction in shipping disruptions, and smooth operations.

What should I look for in e-commerce order tracking software?

For e-commerce teams, the most important features are: branded tracking page on your own domain (not the carrier’s generic page), proactive exception notifications triggered on carrier scan events, self-serve order lookup embeddable on your storefront, multi-carrier support covering your full carrier mix, integration with Klaviyo or your existing email/SMS platform, and delivery analytics for carrier performance monitoring.
