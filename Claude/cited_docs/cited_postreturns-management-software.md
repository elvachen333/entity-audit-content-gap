# The Best Returns Management Software Solutions & How to Improve the Process

The returns process is a critical touchpoint for customer satisfaction and business efficiency. With consumers returning nearly 30% of online purchases, how a company manages returns can significantly impact its reputation and bottom line.

Returns management software has become a game-changer, offering businesses the tools to automate and streamline return processes, ultimately enhancing the customer experience. It’s important to remember that customers making [product returns](https://www.extensiv.com/blog/product-returns) are not lost opportunities for future sales, and optimizing the returns process makes a huge difference for customer retention.

A top-tier returns management system can solve headaches in the entire return process, while the wrong one can aggravate matters that already plague you. The right system can provide valuable insights into return trends and customer behavior—and give customers more control and flexibility over how they make and monitor returns status with notifications and apps, while the wrong one can confuse customer support, service teams, and decision-makers.

### Loop

![Loop Returns](https://www.extensiv.com/hubfs/loop-isologo.svg)

[Loop](https://www.loopreturns.com/) offers a user-friendly customer returns portal, enhancing the brand experience with customizable interfaces. Its technology seamlessly integrates with existing systems via convenient APIs, supporting various return options, including mail-in and in-store for retailers. However, its focus on specific market segments may limit its applicability for all businesses.

Pros: Loop excels in technology integration, offering a seamless connection with ecommerce platforms. Its customer portal is notably user-friendly, enhancing the overall returns experience.

Cons: The platform might require a learning curve for businesses to leverage its customization capabilities fully.

### Happy Returns

![Happy Returns](https://www.extensiv.com/hs-fs/hubfs/HR%2BUPS%2BLogo%2Bcircle@2x.png?width=478&height=100&name=HR%2BUPS%2BLogo%2Bcircle@2x.png)

[Happy Returns](https://happyreturns.com/) excels in providing hassle-free return experiences with extensive drop-off locations, fostering customer loyalty. While its innovative approach promotes sustainability initiatives, the requirement for physical presence might not suit all ecommerce models that only operate as online retailers.

Pros: This solution stands out for its flexibility in return options, including mail-in, in-store, and drop-off, complemented by easy shipping label generation. It also offers excellent tracking and visibility.

Cons: Limited customization options for the user interface may not fully align with all brand aesthetics or support the fully branded returns options you may want.

### Returns Rabbit

![Return Rabbit](https://www.extensiv.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Return%20Rabbit.png?width=368&height=100&name=Return%20Rabbit.png)

[Return Rabbit](https://returnrabbit.com/) specializes in automating refunds and exchanges, offering robust tracking and visibility to streamline the returns process. Its limitations may include a steeper learning curve for new users and integration challenges with less common ecommerce platforms.

Pros: Return Rabbit provides robust restocking and inventory management features, ensuring returned items are efficiently processed and accounted for.

Cons: It might be less intuitive for customers unfamiliar with digital returns processes, potentially affecting the user experience.

### AfterShip Returns

![AfterShip Returns](https://www.extensiv.com/hs-fs/hubfs/aftership%20logo.png?width=400&height=100&name=aftership%20logo.png)

[AfterShip](https://www.aftership.com/) stands out for its comprehensive tracking and visibility features, supporting informed decision-making. Though it offers extensive capabilities, some businesses might find its cost management insights need more depth for complex analyses.

Pros: AfterShip is renowned for its comprehensive tracking and visibility, both internally for businesses and externally for customers, enhancing transparency in the returns process.

Cons: While strong in tracking returns, it may require additional integration efforts for complete inventory management solutions.

### Narvar

![Narvar](https://www.extensiv.com/hs-fs/hubfs/narvar%20logo.png?width=370&height=100&name=narvar%20logo.png)

[Narvar](https://corp.narvar.com/) caters to larger brands with a suite of advanced features, including sophisticated reporting and inventory management. Its focus on big brands means small to medium enterprises might not find it as accessible or cost-effective.

Pros: Narvar is ideal for brands with complex workflows, offering extensive customization of the customer portal and advanced reporting and cost management insights.

Cons: Its sophisticated platform might present less affordable pricing and a higher complexity barrier for smaller ecommerce operations.

### ClickPost

[ClickPost Returns](https://www.clickpost.ai/) simplifies the complexities of returns management by providing a branded, self-service portal that enables customers to initiate returns effortlessly. With automated return approval workflows and real-time tracking, it reduces manual intervention, helping brands handle high return volumes efficiently while encouraging exchanges over refunds.

Pros: This platform excels in offering a branded self-service return portal, automated workflows, and real-time tracking and notifications. It also features customizable return policies and an analytics dashboard that empowers businesses with return data insights. Most importantly, it helps keep profits up with automated nudges to increase the conversion of returns to exchanges.

Cons: Some users have noted that ClickPost Returns' visual interface can be complex for newly onboarded end-users, requiring time to navigate and fully utilize all features effectively.
