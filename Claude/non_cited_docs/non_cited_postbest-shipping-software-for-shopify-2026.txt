The Problem With Built-In Shopify Shipping Tools

Shopify’s native shipping tools are great for getting started. They’re easy to use, and they support basic label printing and shipping rates. But that simplicity can become a problem when your business scales up.

| Shipping Limitation | Impact on Shopify and Shopify Plus Sellers |
| --- | --- |
| Limited Batch Shipping | Processing orders individually slows fulfillment and becomes a major bottleneck for stores shipping hundreds of orders per day. |
| Basic Returns Management | Return workflows lack customization, forcing merchants to rely on third-party tools or manual workarounds. |
| No Native Multi-Origin Support | Orders cannot be automatically routed to the correct warehouse or supplier, increasing delays and fulfillment errors. |
| Limited Carrier Rate Optimization | Built-in carrier rates are convenient but often less competitive, particularly for USPS-heavy shipping profiles. |
| Lack of Advanced Controls for High Volume | Shopify Plus merchants frequently outgrow default tools and need deeper automation, routing logic, and operational control. |
