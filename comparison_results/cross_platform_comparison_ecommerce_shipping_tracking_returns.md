## Executive Summary

All three AI platforms agree on one thing: **pricing specificity is non-negotiable**. Beyond that, each platform has a distinct editorial personality that rewards different content decisions. Writing one article that performs on all three requires deliberately layering signals — some universal, some platform-specific.

The single most efficient win: **add a FAQ section and use "Key Features" headers**. These are Claude-critical, cost nothing on Google AIO or ChatGPT, and add genuine user value. Combined with pricing tables and "Best for" labels, this template covers the majority of signals across all three platforms.

## 2. Platform-Specific Signals — Tune Per Platform

### 2.1 The Location divergence — the biggest strategic split

This is the starkest disagreement between platforms and the single most important platform-specific decision:

| Platform | Location Coverage Lift | Location Density Lift | Verdict |
|----------|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Google AIO** | **+1.14x** | **+13.7x** | ✅ Must include — weave throughout |
| ChatGPT | −0.88x | moderate | ❌ Skip structured tables |
| Claude | −0.76x | **−0.41x** | ❌❌ Actively harmful |

**Implication:** If writing one article for all three platforms, include geographic market information (carrier availability, country support) but keep it **inline as a field in each tool section**, not as a dedicated geographic table or a repeated theme. This satisfies Google AIO without triggering the negative signal on Claude and ChatGPT.
